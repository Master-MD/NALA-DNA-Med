# Legal and Governance Draft

Status: 2026-05-27

This is not legal advice. A lawyer should review the final license, foundation model, trademark language, patent language, app disclaimer, and any clinical-data workflow before public medical or commercial use.

## Important Tension

The project has two goals:

- stay open source and useful for students, doctors, researchers, and public-interest builders
- make sure large commercial profits can support a future global-health foundation or fund

These goals need careful legal design. A standard open-source license such as Apache-2.0 generally allows broad commercial use. If the license requires revenue sharing or forbids fields of use, it may no longer qualify as open source under the Open Source Definition.

Reference links:

- Open Source Definition: https://opensource.org/osd
- Apache License 2.0: https://www.apache.org/licenses/LICENSE-2.0
- GitHub license guidance: https://docs.github.com/en/repositories/managing-your-repositorys-settings-and-features/customizing-your-repository/licensing-a-repository

## Possible Model

- Code license: Apache-2.0 or another OSI-approved license, after legal review.
- Trademark policy: protect `NALA-DNA-Med`, `NALA-AppZ`, and related marks from misleading use.
- Responsible use policy: state unacceptable uses and safety expectations.
- Contributor agreement or DCO: clarify inbound contributions.
- Foundation model: invite or contractually require commercial partners to contribute to a future foundation where legally possible.
- Commercial certification: optional paid certification/support channel for organizations that need compliance and governance.

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not claim `Patent Pending` unless a patent application has actually been filed.
- Do not say `All rights reserved` in the same file as an Apache-style open-source grant without legal review.
- Do not promise that revenue sharing is enforceable through an open-source license without legal review.
- Do not accept real patient data before privacy, consent, security, and governance are designed.

## App Agreement Draft Direction

The first-run agreement should cover:

- research-support only
- no medical advice
- no diagnosis or treatment
- no emergency use
- no illegal, harmful, or abusive use
- no biological/chemical harm workflows
- user responsibility for uploaded data
- third-party model/source licenses
- no warranty
- local/private data explanation
- consent requirement for clinical data
